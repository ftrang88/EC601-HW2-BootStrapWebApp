Who I am

While the current focus of modern cancer research is on the discovery of more effective therapies, the resulting increase in survival, particularly in pediatric patients, opens new concerns regarding quality of life post-treatment. As a cancer survivor, I have a deep appreciation for the science and breakthroughs that have led to my own recovery. However the large number of survivors with long-term and often debilitating treatment-related side-effects highlights the need to address cancer in a broader context beyond simply defeating the disease. With background in biological cancer research, I aim to approach the problems of cancer detection and treatment from an engineering perspective.
